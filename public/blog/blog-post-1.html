2024 FMCSA Compliance Changes Every Carrier Must Know

January 15, 2024 • 5 min read

As we enter 2024, motor carriers face several important regulatory changes that impact daily operations and compliance requirements. Understanding these changes early helps you adapt smoothly and avoid penalties.

Electronic Logging Device (ELD) Enhancements

The FMCSA continues to refine ELD requirements with new technical specifications. Carriers must ensure their ELD providers have updated to the latest firmware versions and that all drivers understand the new personal conveyance guidelines clarified in recent rulings.

Drug & Alcohol Clearinghouse Query Requirements

Annual query requirements are now strictly enforced with enhanced penalties for non-compliance. Carriers must maintain documentation of all limited queries conducted for current employees. The clearinghouse database has also implemented improved search capabilities to prevent circumvention.

Insurance Minimum Requirements

Liability insurance minimum amounts have been under review, with proposals to increase requirements for certain carrier classes. While final rules haven't been published, carriers should work with insurance providers to understand potential impacts on coverage needs and costs.

Hours of Service Flexibility

The HOS flexibility provisions introduced in previous years remain in effect. Understanding how to properly use the short-haul exception, adverse driving conditions exception, and split sleeper-berth provisions can significantly improve operational efficiency while maintaining safety.

Safety Measurement System Updates

SMS scores calculation methodology has been refined to better reflect carrier safety performance. The FMCSA uses newer data more heavily in calculations, emphasizing recent compliance history over older violations. Carriers should regularly monitor their scores through the SMS portal.

Action Steps for Carriers

  • Review and update all driver training materials to reflect current regulations
  • Verify ELD provider compliance with latest technical specifications
  • Establish calendar reminders for annual clearinghouse queries
  • Consult with insurance agent about coverage adequacy
  • Monitor SMS scores monthly and address adverse trends immediately
